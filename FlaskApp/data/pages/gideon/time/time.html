Eid Al-Fitr will officially start on Monday May 2

Ramadan will end tomorrow night


by Sophia Dyer April 30, 2022


Eid Al-Fitr will officially begin on Monday May 2.


Sunday May 1 will be the last day of Ramadan 2022, and Eid-Al-Fitr will start on Monday May 2, which is the first day of the month of Shawwal 1443 AH.


The UAE’s Moon Sighting Committee met on Saturday evening and declared that the moon could not be spotted, so Ramadan 2022 will officially end on Sunday evening.


You might also like: See new shows at Burj Khalifa and The Dubai Fountain for Eid 2022


There will be a series of firework displays across the city this weekend to celebrate (find out where to watch them here).


This week’s Eid break will start on Monday May 2 and last until the evening of Wednesday May 4 – meaning a three-day holiday for those who work Monday to Friday.


Meanwhile, a nine-day holiday was announced for the public sector, beginning on Friday May 29. And it was announced that schools would have the week off (from Monday May 2 to Monday May 9).


The government announced Eid Al Fitr COVID-19 protocols, while malls have announced special opening times to serve Eid shoppers.
